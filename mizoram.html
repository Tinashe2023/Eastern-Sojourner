Getting There & Around

By Air

Lengpui Airport (AJL) near Aizawl connects to Kolkata, Guwahati and Imphal.

Airport to Aizawl: ~1 hour by road

By Train

Nearest broad-gauge access via Silchar (Assam). Bairabi station (within Mizoram) has limited services.

Silchar to Aizawl: ~6-7 hours by road

By Road

Good road connectivity from Silchar (Assam). Mountainous roads—plan extra travel time.

NH 306 connects to Aizawl

Essential Travel Tips

Inner Line Permit (ILP)

Mandatory for Indian citizens (non-residents) visiting Mizoram

  • Apply via Mizoram facilitation counters; available at Lengpui Airport
  • Foreigners: No PAP for most areas; register within 24 hours
  • Carry multiple ID copies and passport-size photos
  • Validity varies by category; extensions possible in Aizawl

Health & Safety

Standard precautions for hill travel

  • Motion sickness meds for winding roads
  • Drink bottled/boiled water only
  • Medical facilities limited outside Aizawl
  • Travel insurance recommended

Best Practices

Make your trip smoother

  • Book stays in advance (limited inventory in season)
  • Start early for day trips (roads are slow)
  • Respect church services on Sundays; many shops close
  • Carry cash; ATMs sparse outside Aizawl

Connectivity

Network can be patchy on hill routes

  • Airtel, Jio work best in Aizawl
  • Download offline maps
  • Inform family about limited contact on road days
  • WiFi may be slow in rural stays

Must-Visit Attractions

Reiek

A scenic hill 29 km from Aizawl with sweeping views over the hills and the plains of Bangladesh on clear days; venue of the Anthurium Festival.

Viewpoints Trekking Photography
~29 km from Aizawl

Hmuifang

A forested plateau ~50 km from Aizawl known for pristine woods, meadows and short village walks; popular for picnics and festivals.

Nature Picnic Culture
~50 km from Aizawl

Vantawng Falls

Mizoram’s highest waterfall near Thenzawl (Serchhip district), cascading through dense forest—best seen after the monsoon.

Waterfall Viewing Point Nature
Near Thenzawl, Serchhip

Tam Dil

A serene lake near Saitual (~85 km from Aizawl) famed for boating and lakeside picnics amid wooded hills; name means “Lake of Mustard Plant”.

Lake Boating Picnic
Saitual district

Suggested Itineraries

3-4 Days Aizawl Essentials

Perfect for first-time visitors focusing on Aizawl and nearby day trips.

Day 1

Arrival & Aizawl City

  • Morning: Arrive at Lengpui, drive to Aizawl
  • Check-in and lunch
  • State Museum, Solomon's Temple
  • Evening: Bara Bazar walk
Day 2

Reiek Day Trip

  • Drive to Reiek; short hike to viewpoint
  • Village visit, local lunch
  • Golden hour photography
  • Return to Aizawl
Day 3

Hmuifang or Vantawng

  • Option A: Hmuifang meadows & picnic
  • Option B: Thenzawl & Vantawng Falls
  • Dinner back in Aizawl
Day 4

Departure

  • Souvenir shopping
  • Optional: Aizawl Cathedral
  • Drive to Lengpui Airport
