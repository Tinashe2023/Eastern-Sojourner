Getting There & Around

By Air

Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International Airport in Guwahati. Well connected to major Indian cities and some international destinations.

Airport to City: 30-40 minutes

By Train

Guwahati Railway Station is a major junction. Well connected to Kolkata, Delhi, Bangalore, and other cities.

Regular trains from major cities

By Road

Well-connected by National Highways. State and private buses available from neighboring states.

NH27, NH37 major routes

Weather & Climate

Winter (Nov - Feb)

10°C - 25°C

Pleasant and dry. Best time for wildlife safaris and tea estate visits.

Pack: Light woolens, comfortable shoes

Summer (Mar - May)

24°C - 36°C

Hot and humid. Early mornings and evenings are pleasant for sightseeing.

Pack: Cotton clothes, sunscreen, hat

Monsoon (Jun - Sep)

26°C - 32°C

Heavy rainfall. Brahmaputra floods possible. Wildlife parks may close.

Pack: Raincoat, waterproof bags

Essential Travel Tips

No Special Permits

Unlike other Northeast states, Assam doesn't require ILP for Indian tourists

  • Foreign tourists need Protected Area Permit (PAP) for some areas
  • Available at Foreigners Registration Office (FRO)
  • Most tourist areas accessible without permits

Wildlife Safari Tips

Maximize your Kaziranga experience

  • Book safaris in advance (online or at park)
  • Morning safaris (5:30 AM) best for wildlife
  • Elephant safaris offer close encounters
  • Carry binoculars and good camera

Tea Estate Visits

Experience Assam's tea heritage

  • Visit heritage tea estates in Jorhat, Dibrugarh
  • Book plantation tours in advance
  • Best time: February to May (plucking season)
  • Try fresh Assam tea at local stalls

River Travel

Brahmaputra and island visits

  • Ferry services available to Majuli Island
  • Sunset cruises on Brahmaputra from Guwahati
  • Check ferry schedules in advance
  • Avoid monsoon season for river travel

Safety Precautions

Stay safe during your travels

  • Avoid isolated areas after dark
  • Be cautious during monsoon floods
  • Wildlife safety: maintain distance from animals
  • Keep emergency contacts handy

Cultural Etiquette

Respect local customs

  • Remove shoes before entering temples
  • Dress modestly at religious sites
  • Ask permission before photographing locals
  • Try local Assamese cuisine respectfully

Must-Visit Attractions

Majuli Island

World's largest river island on the Brahmaputra. Famous for Vaishnavite monasteries (Satras), mask-making, traditional pottery, and rich cultural heritage.

Cultural Island Heritage
347 km from Guwahati, ferry from Jorhat

Tea Gardens

Sprawling tea estates in Jorhat, Dibrugarh, and Tinsukia. Experience tea plucking, processing, and tasting. Stay at heritage tea bungalows for authentic experience.

Tea Tourism Plantation Heritage
Various locations in Upper Assam

Kamakhya Temple

Ancient Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Kamakhya atop Nilachal Hill. One of the 51 Shakti Peethas. Famous for Ambubachi Mela festival in June.

Religious Ancient Pilgrimage
Guwahati (8 km from city center)

Hotel Booking Tips

Book Early for Bihu

Hotels fill up during Rongali Bihu (mid-April). Book 1-2 months in advance.

Kaziranga Accommodation

Stay near the park for early morning safaris. Limited options, book well in advance.

Tea Estate Bungalows

Heritage tea bungalows offer unique stays. Book through tea estate websites directly.

Budget Options

Guwahati has many budget hotels near railway station. Check reviews before booking.

Suggested Itineraries

3-4 Days Guwahati & Kaziranga

Perfect introduction to Assam covering the capital and world-famous wildlife sanctuary.

Day 1

Arrival & Guwahati Sightseeing

  • Morning: Arrive in Guwahati, check-in to hotel
  • Visit Kamakhya Temple (early morning to avoid crowds)
  • Afternoon: Brahmaputra River cruise
  • Evening: Fancy Bazar market, local dinner
Day 2

Drive to Kaziranga

  • Morning: Drive to Kaziranga (4-5 hours, 217 km)
  • Check-in to hotel/resort near the park
  • Afternoon: Rest and explore nearby villages
  • Evening: Safari briefing, early night for morning safari
Day 3

Kaziranga Wildlife Safari

  • Early morning: Elephant/Jeep safari (5:30 AM)
  • Breakfast at lodge
  • Afternoon: Second safari (different zone)
  • Evening: Wildlife photography, bird watching
Day 4

Return to Guwahati & Departure

  • Optional: Early morning safari
  • Drive back to Guwahati
  • Last-minute shopping for Assam tea
  • Departure from Guwahati

Kaziranga National Park

UNESCO World Heritage Site, home to the world's largest population of one-horned rhinoceros. Also houses tigers, elephants, wild buffalo, and over 500 bird species.

Wildlife UNESCO Site Safari
217 km from Guwahati (4-5 hours)
